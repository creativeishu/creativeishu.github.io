About me

I'm a post-doctoral research associate at Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory (Fermilab), Greater Chicago Area. My research interests are in machine learning, deep neural networks, artificial intelligence and computational sciences. I have an extremely strong background in mathematics, physics and statistics with more than five years of experience in computer programming.

I hold a PhD in Physics from University of Zurich in Switzerland, where I studied large scale structures of the Universe and gravitational lensing. I also have an M.S. in Astrophysics from University of Bonn in Germany.

I hold visiting researcher position at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (Berkeley Lab) and associate fellow position at Kavli Institute for Cosmological Physics, University of Chicago. I have collaborated with various reputed institutions like IBM Watson Research Center in New York, Tata Institute for Fundamental Physics (TIFR) in Mumbai India and University of Minnesota, Minneapolis.

I advise start-ups and companies on building and scaling data science and machine learning teams. I strongly believe that my skills are transferable to any data-driven organization with an emphasis on research. Email me for consulation and discussion. I'm also available for speaking.

My
Experience

  • November 2015 - Present

    Fermilab

    Associate Research Scientist

    The current focus is on the applications of machine learning, deep neural networks, dimensional reduction, network analysis and large volume simulations in various cosmological problems like tomographic analysis of weak lensing shear, galaxy networks, dark matter clustering, gravitational lensing etc.

  • January 2014 - Present

    Berkeley Lab

    Visiting Researcher

    Developed an analytic model for the clustering of matter in the Universe. This model predicts observations and simulations with 1-2% accuracy which is 5 times better than previous studies. Also wrote an open source Fortran90 and Python implementation of the model. The results were published in a scientific journal.

  • October 2012 - September 2015

    University of Zurich

    Graduate Researcher

    Employed numerical methods, computer programming (Python, Fortran90, C++, Matlab etc.), statistical inference (MCMC methods, Fisher analysis, genetic algorithm, hypothesis testing etc.), mathematics (linear algebra, calculus, differential equations), machine learning (random forests, kNN, boosted trees, clustering) to develop models for the statistical properties of the distribution of matter in the Universe, forecasts for future generation surveys, and applications of novel algorithms in cosmology. This work resulted in 10 scientific publications and a Ph.D. thesis.

  • October 2010 - September 2012

    University of Bonn

    Research Assistant

    Pursued research to forecast the cosmological results of the X-ray satellite telescope eRosita (German and Russian mission) using Fisher matrix analysis and Markov-Chain Monte-Carlo (MCMC). Submitted a master’s thesis on this research.
